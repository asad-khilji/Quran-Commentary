Surah Al Fatihah: Ayah 1

By: Nouman Ali Khan

Introduction

  • Surah Al Fatihah was revealed in Makkah
  • The rest of the Quran is a Tafseer of the Fatihah
  • Surah Al Fatihah begins with Alhamdulillah
  • Bismillah is not part of the Surah

Hamd

  • Hamd means praise and thanks
  • Praise and Thanks are two different ideas
  • You can praise without thanking and you can thank without praising
  • Musa thanks the Pharoah for raising him, but he does not praise him as a god
  • Ibrahim thanks his father for raising him, but he does not praise his worship of the idols
  • When we say Alhamdulillah, we have two attitudes in mind praise and thanks
  • When we praise someone and something we should not complain
  • We should find a genuine and sincere reason to praise and thank Allah
  • Our deen begins with optimism and a positive attitude
  • This is a complete change in thought and attitude
  • When you are constantly positive then it is easy to praise and thank Allah
  • When you are constantly negative then it is difficult to praise and thank Allah
  • Hamd is genuine and sincere praise and thanks
  • Praise and Thanks is limited to a reaction
  • Hamd is praise and thanks that is not limited to a reaction

Nouns and Verbs

  • Nouns are timeless and independent
  • Verbs are temporary and dependent
  • Hamd is a noun
  • The praise and thanks for Allah is timeless and independent from anyone or anything
  • Allah is not in need of our praise and thanks
  • Whether we praise and thank Allah or not the Hamd of Allah still exists

Informational and Emotional Sentences

  • Informational Sentences are sentences whose purpose is information
  • Emotional Sentences are sentences whose purpose is emotion
  • Allah says, ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ so it is a sentence of information and emotion
  • If Allah said, Innil Alhamdulillah then it would be informational sentence only

Surah Al Fatihah and Surah Al Jathiya

  • Surah Al Fatihah says لْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ or Praise and Thanks is for Allah
  • Surah Al Jathiya says فَلِلَّهِ ٱلْحَمْدُ or Praise and Thanks is only for Allah
  • فَلِلَّهِ ٱلْحَمْدُ is only used when arguing with someone
  • In Surah Al Fatihah we already know who Allah is
  • Surah Al Fatihah is a statement from the heart and not arguing with anyone
  • Surah Al Jathiya is arguing with the polytheists

The Name of Allah

  • When we call Allah by His Name than we appreciate all the names and qualities of Allah
  • We can praise Allah with one word and include all His attributes by using the name Allah
  • Allah is His proper name
  • Names are very important in an introduction
  • Allah introduces Himself to us in Surah Al Fatihah so He uses His name
  • By Allah using His name it clears any confusion of Hamd being for anyone else

The Ultimate Praise and Thanks

  • Praise and Thanks could be for other people and things
  • But ultimately praise and thanks goes back to Allah

The Ultimate, Genuine, Sincere, & Timeless Praise and Thanks is for Allah who is independent from His creation

Rabb

  • Malik means Master or Owner
  • Sayyed means the one who is in control and in charge
  • Murrabi means the one who is improving us constantly and allows us to grow & develop
  • Qayyim means the one who sustains us and takes care of us constantly
  • Munnim means the one who gives and gifts over and over again

The Master and Slave Relationship

  • Allah is our Master and we are His Slaves
  • Human beings have a negative connotation of slavery
  • But Allah is a unique Master
  • Human beings have this delusion that they are free
  • There is no such thing as freedom
  • We are enslaved to someone or something such as money, fashion, government, etc
  • When you enslave yourself to Allah then you are free from all other forms of slavery

Alameen

  • For every nation
  • For every people
  • For every generation
  • For every culture
  • In every time
  • In every place
  • Those people who need a warning
